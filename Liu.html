Overview
01

Liu Harper is a central figure across the HourGlass universe, representing the mind within the core symbolic triad alongside Jackson (the body) and Moxie (the soul). He serves as a background antagonist in HourGlass: Missing Time and a conflicted protagonist in HourGlass: Lost Time.

His complicity in the M.A.T.C.H. system's expansion — whether through direct action or willful silence — forms the moral weight at the center of the HourGlass narrative. By Lost Time, he has returned under circumstances he does not fully understand, haunted by what he allowed to happen.

"I didn't build the system. I just never stopped it."
— Recovered audio, Lost Time  //  Timestamp withheld
Role in Missing Time
02

Liu does not appear physically during Missing Time's main storyline. His influence is documented across recovered MEK-A personnel files and the MEK-AI terminal, where his authorization signatures appear on facility protocols and containment orders. The nature of his absence during the events of the game is not explained within Missing Time itself.

Players investigating the terminal at L2 clearance will find partial records referencing Harper's access to sub-level systems well before the Diner opened publicly. His signature appears on at least three containment orders, including the one cross-referenced in INC-0019.

Role in Lost Time
03

In Lost Time, control shifts to Liu directly. He has returned — though he does not know why or how — and must help three kidnapped individuals escape the system he once upheld. The game explores his guilt and his fractured understanding of what M.A.T.C.H. became after his disappearance.
